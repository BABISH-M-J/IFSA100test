IFSA has verified that each of its housing options has planned for the pandemic and will activate pandemic procedures upon the recommendation of local and national public health authorities. However, it is important to acknowledge that shared spaces increase one’s exposure to illness and thus, risk. We are unable to guarantee that a student will not be exposed to illness on an IFSA program or in IFSA-arranged housing, and we strongly recommend that students comply with recommended hygiene measures and PPE standards as recommended by public health authorities in their program location.

Some considerations when choosing housing may include, but are not limited to:

  • A double room with shared bathroom maximizes your exposure to shared spaces. Should your roommate become ill, it is likely that you will also be required to quarantine. Similarly, should your roommate undertake risky travel or other behavior, you may be impacted by this.
  • A single room with shared bathroom does not eliminate risk, but minimizes it further, particularly if students engage in enhanced hygiene practices. A shared bathroom may still lead to a shared quarantine.
  • A single room with en-suite bathroom minimizes risk, but still does not eliminate it. Shared spaces may still include hallways, kitchens, and other common areas. Students may, depending on local government regulations and/or housing provider policy, be able to self-isolate without moving out of their room.
  • Students in homestays should be prepared to function as part of the family unit, including for the purpose of quarantine and isolation measures. Students will eat meals with and share space with the family. If one member becomes sick, each may become sick, and the family may need to isolate as a unit. All families are prepared to care for a student who becomes ill.
